# Workforce Framework for Cybersecurity (NICE Framework)
**NIST Special Publication** | NIST SP 800-181r1 | 2020-11

This publication from the National Initiative for Cybersecurity Education (NICE) describes the Workforce Framework for Cybersecurity (NICE Framework), a fundamental reference for describing and sharing information about cybersecurity work. It expresses that work as Task statements and describes Knowledge and Skill statements that provide a foundation for learners including students, job seekers, and employees. As a common, consistent lexicon that categorizes and describes cybersecurity work, the NICE Framework improves communication about how to identify, recruit, develop, and retain cybersecurity talent.

---

## Framework Overview
### purpose
Provide a common language for describing cybersecurity work and the people who perform it, supporting workforce planning, education, training, and recruitment

### applicability
All organizations — public sector, private, nonprofit, education, training providers

### audience
['Public sector agencies', 'Private and not-for-profit organizations', 'Education and training providers', 'Curriculum developers', 'Credential providers', 'Human resource professionals', 'Hiring managers', 'Workforce planners', 'Recruiters', 'Line managers', 'All learners (students, job seekers, employees)']

### terminology_note
Those performing cybersecurity work — including students, job seekers, and employees — are referenced as Learners throughout

## Building Blocks
### overview
The NICE Framework is built upon discrete building blocks that describe the work to be done (Tasks) and what is required to perform that work (Knowledge and Skills). These are guidelines to enhance comprehension rather than rigid structures.
